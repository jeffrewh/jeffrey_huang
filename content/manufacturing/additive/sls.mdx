## 3. Powder Bed Fusion (e.g., SLS/SLM)

[cite_start]**Powder Bed Fusion** processes use a high-energy source, like a laser or an electron beam, to selectively fuse regions of a powder bed [cite: 7246-7247]. The two main types are **Selective Laser Sintering (SLS)**, which typically uses polymers, and **Selective Laser Melting (SLM)** or **Direct Metal Laser Sintering (DMLS)**, which are used for metals.

The process takes place inside a chamber. A roller or blade spreads an extremely thin layer of powder (e.g., titanium, aluminum, nylon) over a build platform. A powerful laser then scans the cross-section of the part, melting and fusing the powder particles together. [cite_start]The platform then lowers, a new layer of powder is applied, and the process repeats [cite: 7675-7686].

[cite_start]The physics are complex, involving rapid melting and resolidification that creates unique material **microstructures** [cite: 7897-7902].
